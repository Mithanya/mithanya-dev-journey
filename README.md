# Still Not Placed in 2026 – My Real Journey

## Overview
This repository contains a personal blog that reflects my experience as a final-year engineering student navigating the placement process in 2026. It is an honest account of challenges, mistakes, learning, and continuous effort toward building a career in the IT field.

## About the Blog
The blog focuses on the reality of not being placed during campus recruitment and the internal and external pressures that come with it. Instead of presenting a success story, it documents the process of growth, persistence, and self-improvement while still being in the middle of the journey.

## Key Themes
- Placement pressure in final year
- Handling rejection and uncertainty
- Observing peers getting placed
- Self-reflection and identifying mistakes
- Transition from passive learning to active building
- Consistent effort without waiting for readiness

## What I Learned
- Clarity of direction is more important than learning multiple topics superficially  
- Building projects is more valuable than only completing tutorials  
- Applying early is better than waiting to feel fully prepared  
- Rejection should be treated as feedback, not failure  

## Current Focus
- Strengthening fundamentals in programming and problem solving  
- Building real-world projects that demonstrate practical skills  
- Applying consistently to opportunities  
- Documenting the journey to maintain accountability and growth  

## Purpose of This Repository
This repository serves as:
- A record of my career journey  
- A demonstration of communication and reflection skills  
- A way to share a real and relatable experience with others in similar situations
